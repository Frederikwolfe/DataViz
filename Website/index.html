March Madness Visualizations

Introduction

March Madness is one of the most exciting times of the year for basketball fans. The NCAA Men's Basketball Tournament brings together 64 teams from across the country to compete for the national championship. This page presents a series of visualizations that explore various aspects of the tournament's history, from matchups and upsets to fan predictions and championship leaders.

March Madness Matchups by Seed

The first visualization shows the matchups between different seeds in the tournament from 1985 to 2024. The arcs represent the number of games played between each pair of seeds, with thicker lines indicating more frequent matchups. Light blue lines highlight first-round matchups, providing a clear view of the early tournament dynamics.

March Madness Upsets

Upsets are a crucial part of the excitement in March Madness. This area chart tracks upsets from 2008 to 2024, defined as wins by teams seeded two or more spots below their opponents. The visualization highlights key moments, such as the cancellation of the 2020 tournament due to the COVID-19 pandemic and the record number of upsets in 2016.

The Public's Picks

Fan predictions add another layer of intrigue to the tournament. This line graph shows the percentage of fan brackets that picked each team to win their games in ESPN's Men's Tournament Challenge. The lines track the public's picks through each round, revealing how fan confidence shifts as the tournament progresses.

March Madness Matchups: Seed-by-Seed

This matrix provides an in-depth look at each seed's performance against every other seed from 1985 to 2024. Win percentages are calculated by the columns, and first-round matchups are highlighted. The visualization helps identify which seeds tend to perform well against others, offering insights into historical trends and potential future outcomes.

National Championship Leaders

Finally, this stacked bar chart visualizes the success of top programs in the tournament. It shows the number of national championships, runner-up finishes, and appearances in the Final Four, Elite Eight, and Sweet Sixteen for each team. UCLA leads with the most championships, highlighting their historical dominance in college basketball.
